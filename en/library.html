SHELF

Fragments of an Unlived Fate

Sometimes life doesn’t break.
It simply doesn’t come together — piece by piece, decision by decision, moment by moment.

Without drama.
Without a final point.

There are places where you could have turned.
Seconds when the choice was already made,
even if you didn’t realize it yet.

At some point you begin to understand:
it’s not that the story ended.
It’s that it never came together.

Everything felt like life.
Depth, where choices were made without thinking they could be final.
Heat, where illusions felt like freedom.
Love — so beautiful that you wanted to believe in it more than in yourself.

There were no catastrophes in these stories.
No villains.
Almost no mistakes.

There were moments when you went a little further than you should have.
Or stopped — too early.

The strangest thing is that the women in these stories don’t disappear.
Something else does:
the context in which you mattered,
the role you were expected to play,
the feeling that it was all leading somewhere.

And when the scenery fades,
what remains is silence.
Not empty.
Just honest.

And at some point it becomes clear:
nothing broke.
It simply never happened.

From these pauses
the fragments of an unlived fate are made.

Asia. Women

Asia always begins the same way.
With heat, запахи, movement —
and the feeling that everything can be different here.

Here it’s easy to become
who you once wanted to be.

But at some point
it stops being a background
and becomes a mirror.

In Asia, everything happens faster.
Not because people rush — on the contrary,
they are in no hurry at all.

You simply become lighter.
Less tied to the past,
less bound to the future.

Here you can disappear for a few days —
and it won’t seem strange.
You can return again —
and be welcomed
as if you had never left.

I remember one evening.
We sat on plastic chairs by the road,
drinking something sweet with ice,
and she calmly spoke about her family —
without complaints, without drama.

I listened and caught a strange feeling:
I felt good not because I was with a woman,
but because I was back in context.

I was needed again.
Not as a person —
but as movement.
As possibility.

And for the first time, a question appears
that is never asked out loud:

what will remain,
if this city,
this evening
and this woman disappear at once?
